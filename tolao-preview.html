The Problem

Private-party sourcing breaks down in the first 15 minutes.

Most teams do not lose good cars at appraisal. They lose them earlier, when listings are missed, responses lag, or buyers waste time sorting low-signal leads.

Fresh listings get missed

Good inventory moves quickly, especially when buyers are juggling appraisal, transport, and follow-up.

First response is inconsistent

If outreach timing depends on who is available, coverage breaks down and seller response rates suffer.

Buyers spend time on noise

Too much energy goes into raw listings instead of reviewing opportunities that are already partially qualified.

Good cars disappear early

By the time a human gets to the lead, the seller may already be in motion with someone else.
