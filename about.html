Why we built CarLlama

Private-party sellers deserve the same fair, data-driven pricing that dealers get at auction. We built CarLlama to close that gap.

The problem

If you've ever tried to sell your car on Craigslist or Facebook Marketplace, you know the pattern: 40 messages, 35 lowballers, 3 no-shows, maybe one real buyer three weeks later. Or you take it to a dealer who quotes you trade-in value (below wholesale), then resells it for $5,000 more.

Every step of that process assumes the seller has bad information. It's built on the gap between what a car is actually worth today and what the seller thinks it might be worth.

What we do differently

CarLlama runs your vehicle through the same data streams professional dealers use — Kelley Blue Book retail comps, Manheim auction indices, vAuto live market pricing — and surfaces an honest offer within minutes. Our number reflects current wholesale demand, mileage, trim, condition, and regional market. Not a stale book value from three months ago.

We're not here to trick you into accepting less than the car is worth. We're here to buy the car. If our number makes sense to you, the deal closes in one visit. If it doesn't, no harm done — you walk away with better information than you started with.

How we think about pricing

Our offer isn't retail. A dealer who resells your car has reconditioning costs, lot expenses, advertising, financing overhead, and the risk of holding inventory. That spread — typically 15–20% — is real work, not markup for its own sake.

We price at the wholesale level, the same level dealers bid at auctions, with a modest margin that keeps us in business. For most sellers, that's more than a trade-in quote and considerably less hassle than a private sale. It's the math that matters; we just surface it honestly.
